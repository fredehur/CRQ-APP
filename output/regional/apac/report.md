# APAC Regional Executive Brief — AeroGrid Wind Solutions

**Scenario ID:** APAC-001 | **Department:** Manufacturing | **Severity:** HIGH | **Value at Cyber Risk:** $18,500,000

## Situation Overview

AeroGrid's Asia-Pacific manufacturing operations face a confirmed, high-severity threat of unauthorized system intrusion targeting the region's blade manufacturing plants and proprietary turbine aerodynamic designs. State-sponsored groups operating in the South China Sea corridor are actively pursuing supply chain access to disrupt regional turbine production and extract sensitive manufacturing intellectual property. The confirmed Value at Cyber Risk stands at $18,500,000, representing the assessed maximum financial exposure should this threat materialize against the company's critical assets — specifically, proprietary aerodynamic designs and operational technology networks that govern blade production.

## Risk Context and Empirical Baseline

System intrusion is the single most frequent category of security event globally, accounting for 43.3% of all recorded incidents, and ranks third in total financial impact at 21.4% of aggregate losses. It also accounts for 47.35% of all records affected across industries. For AeroGrid, a successful intrusion into APAC manufacturing facilities would directly threaten the company's ability to fulfill turbine production schedules, protect design intellectual property that underpins its competitive position, and maintain continuity of service to downstream wind farm operators. The $18,500,000 exposure figure reflects the combined cost of production downtime, intellectual property compromise, contractual penalties, and recovery across the APAC footprint.

## Board-Level Implication

This threat directly endangers AeroGrid's core revenue engine — wind turbine manufacturing represents 75% of the company's business. Disruption to APAC blade production would cascade into delayed project deliveries, partner contract penalties, and potential loss of proprietary design advantage that differentiates AeroGrid in the renewable energy market. The board should treat the $18,500,000 at-risk figure as the financial anchor for any resource allocation decisions related to APAC operational resilience.
