# LATAM Regional Cyber Risk Brief

**Region:** Latin America (LATAM) | **Value at Cyber Risk (VaCR):** $8,500,000 | **Severity:** 2/3 — Standard

## Strategic Assessment

A coordinated wave of ransomware activity is disrupting banking infrastructure across Brazil and Mexico, the two largest markets in our LATAM portfolio. Government-linked threat groups have demonstrated the ability to shut down payment processing channels for days at a time, and several regional financial institutions have already experienced service interruptions. Our Payment Gateway and Customer Database are directly exposed to this disruption chain: if our payment processing partners in these markets are degraded or taken offline, we face delayed revenue collection, settlement failures, and erosion of customer trust. Given the $8,500,000 in value-at-cyber-risk attributed to this region, even a partial disruption to our payment infrastructure during peak transaction periods could translate into material revenue loss and contractual penalties with merchant partners.

Compounding this operational risk, currency instability across the region is being amplified by a surge in cyber-enabled financial fraud targeting local banking systems. The combination of macroeconomic pressure and degraded confidence in digital financial infrastructure creates a scenario where our regional operations face dual exposure: direct business interruption from ransomware-driven outages and indirect financial loss from fraud-inflated currency volatility. Organizations that depend on local banking rails for settlement and treasury operations — as we do — are particularly vulnerable to cascading delays that extend well beyond the initial disruption event.

## Recommended Board-Level Posture

The board should treat the LATAM threat environment as elevated. The convergence of state-adjacent ransomware campaigns and macroeconomic fragility means that the full $8,500,000 VaCR is plausibly at risk over the next two quarters. Leadership should ensure that business continuity plans for LATAM payment operations are current, that alternative settlement pathways are identified, and that customer communication protocols are ready to deploy should service degradation occur.
