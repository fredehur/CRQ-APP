# NCE Regional Cyber Risk Brief

**Region:** Northern & Central Europe (NCE) | **Value at Cyber Risk (VaCR):** $33,000,000 | **Severity:** 3/3 — Comprehensive

## Strategic Assessment

Northern and Central Europe faces the most acute operational risk posture of any region this quarter. A sustained, state-directed campaign against energy grid operators across Northern Europe poses a direct and credible threat to the continuity of our Operations Platform and Remote Monitoring System. These are not theoretical risks: the campaign is active, it is targeting infrastructure that our facilities depend on, and disruption to energy distribution would cascade into forced shutdowns and degraded remote oversight of critical operations. The full $33,000,000 in value at cyber risk is exposed if grid-dependent systems lose availability during a prolonged disruption event.

Simultaneously, the enforcement of EU sanctions against Russia has triggered a pattern of retaliatory cyber operations aimed at multinational corporations with visible European footprints. Our operational presence in the NCE region makes us a plausible target for disruptive action intended to impose economic costs on EU-aligned enterprises. The convergence of these two threat streams — state-sponsored infrastructure targeting and sanctions-linked retaliation — creates a compounding risk environment where a single incident could disable both the Operations Platform and Remote Monitoring System at the same time, leaving the organization without the ability to maintain production continuity or real-time asset oversight across the region.

## Recommended Board-Level Posture

Leadership should treat this as a live exposure requiring immediate strategic attention. With NATO's eastern flank on sustained elevated alert and geopolitical tensions showing no sign of de-escalation, the threat window is not closing. The $33,000,000 VaCR figure represents the realistic financial exposure if our NCE operations are disrupted through energy infrastructure compromise or direct retaliatory action. Enterprise resilience planning for this region should be elevated to the highest priority, with clear escalation thresholds tied to the full VaCR exposure to guide executive decision-making.
