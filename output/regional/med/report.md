# MED Regional Executive Brief — AeroGrid Wind Solutions

**Region:** Mediterranean (MED)
**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Severity:** MEDIUM (Score 2 of 3)
**Primary Scenario:** Insider Misuse
**Value at Cyber Risk (VaCR):** $4,200,000

---

Economic instability across southern Europe has elevated the insider misuse threat profile for AeroGrid's Mediterranean operations, specifically within the Service & Maintenance division. The primary exposure centers on the company's real-time predictive maintenance algorithms and offshore servicing contracts — both of which represent core competitive advantages in the region. Should an insider misuse event materialize, the organization faces a quantified Value at Cyber Risk of **$4,200,000**, concentrated in intellectual property loss and potential disruption to contracted offshore wind farm servicing obligations.

Insider misuse ranks **#4 globally by financial impact**, accounting for **4.4% of all recorded losses** across the empirical baseline. While it ranks #5 in event frequency (2.5% of all events), the financial severity per incident is disproportionately high relative to occurrence rates. For AeroGrid, this means a lower-probability but higher-consequence exposure: a single event involving unauthorized access to or exfiltration of predictive maintenance models could compromise the company's ability to deliver contracted service-level commitments to offshore wind operators and erode the proprietary edge that underpins recurring maintenance revenue.

Leadership should recognize that the Mediterranean region's current economic conditions create an environment where personnel with access to high-value service algorithms and contract details present a material risk to business continuity. The $4.2M VaCR figure reflects the potential for direct financial harm to the Service & Maintenance line of business, which represents 25% of AeroGrid's global revenue mix. This exposure warrants board-level visibility as part of the company's ongoing enterprise risk posture review.
