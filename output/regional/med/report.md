# Mediterranean Region (MED) — Executive Threat Brief

**Scenario ID:** MED-001 | **Department:** Service & Maintenance | **Value at Cyber Risk:** $4,200,000 | **Severity:** MEDIUM | **Primary Scenario:** Insider Misuse

## Situation Overview

Economic instability across southern Europe has elevated the risk of corporate espionage and insider misuse targeting AeroGrid Wind Solutions' Mediterranean operations. The $4,200,000 Value at Cyber Risk reflects potential exposure to the Service & Maintenance division, specifically the real-time predictive maintenance algorithms and offshore servicing contracts that underpin the company's competitive position in the region. Empirical data ranks insider misuse as the fourth-highest scenario by financial impact, accounting for 4.4% of all industry losses, with a frequency rank of fifth at 2.5% of all recorded events. This is not a theoretical concern — the economic conditions in the region create measurable incentive for personnel with access to proprietary algorithms to monetize that access through unauthorized disclosure or misuse.

## Business Impact

The critical assets at stake — predictive maintenance algorithms and offshore servicing contracts — represent the operational backbone of AeroGrid's service revenue in the Mediterranean. Compromise of these algorithms would erode the company's ability to deliver differentiated maintenance outcomes to wind farm operators, directly threatening contract renewal and margin. Offshore servicing contracts, often multi-year and high-value, depend on the confidentiality of proprietary scheduling and diagnostic models. A single insider event affecting these assets could trigger contract penalties, loss of competitive advantage, and reputational damage with energy operators across the region, all within the $4,200,000 risk envelope identified by the enterprise risk model.

## Recommendation

Given the medium severity and the direct exposure of revenue-generating service assets, the Mediterranean region warrants standard-depth monitoring with emphasis on access controls around predictive maintenance systems and contract documentation. The board should note that while insider misuse ranks lower in event frequency than external attack scenarios, its financial impact per event is disproportionately high — making this a low-frequency, high-consequence risk that justifies sustained executive attention.
