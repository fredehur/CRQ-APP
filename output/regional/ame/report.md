# AME Regional Threat Brief — AeroGrid Wind Solutions

**Region:** Americas (AME)
**Severity:** CRITICAL
**Primary Scenario:** Ransomware
**Value at Cyber Risk (VaCR):** $22,000,000
**Date:** 2026-03-09

---

AeroGrid's North American Service & Maintenance division faces a critical-severity threat from financially motivated criminal syndicates actively targeting energy sector service providers. The primary risk scenario is Ransomware, which ranks #1 globally for financial impact, accounting for 37.6% of all cyber losses industry-wide. With $22,000,000 in Value at Cyber Risk, a successful disruption would directly impair AeroGrid's ability to receive live telemetry from onshore and offshore wind farms and to coordinate remote maintenance scheduling — the operational backbone of the company's 25% global service revenue stream.

The operational consequence is straightforward: if wind farm telemetry systems are rendered unavailable, AeroGrid loses real-time visibility into turbine performance, fault detection, and predictive maintenance workflows. Maintenance crews cannot be dispatched efficiently, service-level agreements with wind farm operators are breached, and revenue recognition stalls. The $22M exposure reflects not only direct recovery costs but also contractual penalties, lost service revenue during downtime, and reputational damage with utility-scale customers who depend on uninterrupted energy generation.

Given that Ransomware also ranks #2 in event frequency (31.7% of all incidents), this is not a theoretical scenario but a statistically probable one. The AME region should be treated as the highest-priority exposure in this reporting cycle. Executive leadership should be prepared to discuss continuity posture for telemetry-dependent service operations at the next board review.
