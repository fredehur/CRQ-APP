## Executive Summary

AeroGrid faces a combined Value at Cyber Risk exposure of $44,700,000 across three of five global regions, with threats concentrated in the scenarios carrying the highest and third-highest financial impact rankings worldwide. The dominant pattern is geopolitically driven: state-sponsored targeting of manufacturing intellectual property in APAC, financially motivated criminal disruption of service delivery in AME, and insider exploitation of workforce financial stress in the Mediterranean all converge on AeroGrid's crown jewels — proprietary turbine designs, OT manufacturing networks, and live predictive maintenance platforms. Velocity signals across all five regions are stable with no region accelerating or improving over the current observation window, indicating the threat landscape is entrenched rather than in flux. If any one of the three active exposures materializes, the direct consequences range from turbine production throughput losses and IP exfiltration in manufacturing to full service delivery disruption and regulatory penalties across the wind farm operations portfolio.

## Escalated Regions

### AME — $22,000,000 at Risk | Severity: Critical

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Geopolitical

**Primary Scenario:** Ransomware

Regulatory designation of offshore wind operations as critical national infrastructure has placed AeroGrid's AME telemetry and maintenance systems inside a target environment where financially motivated criminal groups are already executing confirmed double-extortion campaigns against comparable wind energy operators. A successful disruption of AeroGrid's live wind farm telemetry or predictive maintenance scheduling would sever remote monitoring capability across the entire AME asset base, producing direct service contract failures and triggering mandatory incident reporting obligations under the new US executive order. With Ransomware ranked first globally by financial impact at 37.6% of all cyber losses, this is the highest-priority single exposure in the current portfolio.

### APAC — $18,500,000 at Risk | Severity: High

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Geopolitical

**Primary Scenario:** System intrusion

Escalating South China Sea territorial disputes and accelerating supply chain diversification pressures have elevated wind energy manufacturing IP to the status of a strategic national asset in the eyes of competing state actors, making AeroGrid's APAC blade production facilities and OT networks a structurally attractive target for state-directed intrusion campaigns. A confirmed supply chain compromise vector through third-party industrial automation software provides persistent access to production line control systems, directly threatening both manufacturing throughput and the proprietary aerodynamic designs embedded in those systems. System intrusion is ranked third globally by financial impact and first by event frequency at 43.3% of all incidents, meaning this exposure combines high financial consequence with the highest probability of occurrence across the scenario register.

### MED — $4,200,000 at Risk | Severity: Medium

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Geopolitical

**Primary Scenario:** Insider misuse

Persistent economic instability across southern Europe is creating financial stress conditions in AeroGrid's Mediterranean service and maintenance workforce, elevating the probability that trusted insiders or third-party contractors will misuse privileged access to predictive maintenance platforms and offshore servicing contract documentation. A confirmed instance of a former contractor retaining active credentials for six months post-termination — with access patterns assessed as data reconnaissance — indicates this exposure has moved from latent workforce risk to active unauthorized presence on production-tier systems. Exfiltration of predictive maintenance algorithms would erode AeroGrid's competitive advantage in service contract renewals across Mediterranean wind markets, while exposure of offshore contract terms could directly benefit competing operators in the same geography.
