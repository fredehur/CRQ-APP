## Escalated Regions

### APAC — $18,500,000 at Risk | Severity: High

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Cyber

**Primary Scenario:** System intrusion

State-sponsored economic competition over renewable energy technology has created durable structural incentives for adversaries to target wind turbine intellectual property, with Iranian state-aligned actor Seedworm corroborated by a multi-agency April 2026 advisory as actively operating against energy operational technology systems — placing AeroGrid's Kaohsiung manufacturing hub, which accounts for 60% of global turbine output and hosts the Series 7 production line and primary SCADA network, directly within the established actor geography. The documented method in the most directly comparable confirmed incident involved digital exfiltration of wind turbine firmware and operational control system code — the same category of proprietary asset held at Kaohsiung — and SideWinder APT's confirmed geographic expansion into Southeast Asia brings additional credential-harvesting tradecraft closer to AeroGrid's regional manufacturing and service supply chain. A successful intrusion would extend consequences beyond internal IP loss to production continuity risk across the TEPCO five-year supply agreement and the Vestas APAC logistics partnership; the current evidence is trend-level rather than incident-specific, meaning the board should treat this as a structural pre-positioning risk rather than a confirmed active campaign, with the 12-run sustained escalation history confirming it is a durable operating condition rather than a transient alert.

### NCE — Watch

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true)

The geopolitical drivers behind intellectual property targeting in Northern and Central Europe are well-corroborated — documented EU-China trade friction, substantial sector-wide losses attributed to technology transfer campaigns, and dominant Chinese production share all establish clear state-level incentives to target European wind manufacturer engineering data. However, no attributed threat actor activity or confirmed operational technology incidents directed at wind sector targets have been detected this cycle, and regional intelligence collection returned no results. The cyber dimension of the risk remains materially undersubstantiated, capping the assessment at monitor status. Severity has oscillated between HIGH and LOW in recent cycles, and velocity is accelerating — the conditions for re-escalation are structurally present even though current intelligence does not confirm an active campaign.
