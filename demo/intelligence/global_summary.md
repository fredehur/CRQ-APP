## Executive Summary

The single most important fact for the board is that three of AeroGrid's five operational regions are simultaneously escalated, with a combined quantified exposure of $44,700,000, and each threat independently targets a distinct crown jewel: proprietary turbine IP in APAC, live wind farm telemetry and maintenance scheduling in AME, and predictive maintenance access in MED. This is a compound risk posture, not three isolated incidents — the convergence of state-directed IP collection, financially motivated extortion against operational platforms, and workforce-driven insider access abuse means AeroGrid faces simultaneous pressure on manufacturing capacity, service delivery continuity, and the integrity of the algorithms that underpin both. All three escalated regions share a cross-cutting pattern: every threat targets turbine operational technology or predictive systems, the same assets that appear in AeroGrid's crown jewel register, indicating that the company's most competitively sensitive capabilities are the consistent point of exposure across geographies and threat types regardless of the actor or method. Global velocity is stable across all five regions and has held at current severity levels across five consecutive pipeline runs, meaning this compound posture is entrenched rather than transient — neither accelerating nor resolving. The practical business consequence is that AeroGrid cannot treat these as sequential issues: a disruption to AME's telemetry-dependent service contracts, a loss of APAC blade production schedules, or an insider exfiltration of MED's maintenance algorithms would each independently affect delivery commitments, and occurring in parallel they represent a material threat to AeroGrid's ability to fulfill turbine manufacturing and global service obligations simultaneously.

## Escalated Regions

### AME — $22,000,000 at Risk | Severity: Critical

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Cyber

**Primary Scenario:** Ransomware

A confirmed extortion campaign against a North American wind energy operator — with a $22,000,000 demand issued and a separate but related attack producing 18 days of operational downtime and a full network rebuild across three facilities — has established a proven methodology directly applicable to AeroGrid's live wind farm telemetry and remote maintenance scheduling platforms. The regulatory environment amplifies the exposure: a US executive order designating offshore wind as critical national infrastructure means a successful disruption now triggers mandatory disclosure obligations alongside the operational damage, raising the total consequence beyond the quantified VaCR figure. With Ransomware ranked first globally by financial impact, this is the highest-consequence scenario in the master register, and the confirmed incident means the attack method has already been proven effective against the wind energy sector's operational architecture.

### APAC — $18,500,000 at Risk | Severity: High

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Geopolitical

**Primary Scenario:** System intrusion

State-directed collection campaigns across Asia-Pacific are specifically targeting wind turbine control systems, predictive maintenance platforms, and blade production infrastructure — assets that map directly to AeroGrid's proprietary aerodynamic designs and the OT/SCADA networks governing blade production in the region. The assessed access vector is supply chain compromise through third-party industrial automation software updates, which would enable persistent presence inside production environments without requiring direct network penetration, and the targeting profile is consistent with strategic technology acquisition driven by South China Sea competition rather than financially motivated disruption. If this sustained collection trend transitions into disruptive operations against production availability, AeroGrid faces potential interruption to blade manufacturing schedules and permanent erosion of the proprietary designs that underpin its competitive position, at a quantified exposure of $18,500,000.

### MED — $4,200,000 at Risk | Severity: Medium

**Intelligence Assessment:** B2 (Usually reliable source — Probably true) | **Trend:** → Stable | **Dominant Pillar:** Geopolitical

**Primary Scenario:** Insider misuse

Sustained economic austerity across southern Europe is creating financial pressure within the field service and maintenance workforce — precisely the personnel who hold privileged access to AeroGrid's predictive maintenance algorithms and offshore servicing contract documentation — and an evidenced incident at a Mediterranean energy operator, where a former maintenance contractor retained active credentials for six months post-departure and accessed wind farm telemetry data, confirms this pathway has already been exploited in the same operating environment. The mixed signal profile — a structural economic trend that has now materialized into a confirmed access event — indicates the risk is not self-correcting, as the conditions driving insider threat motivation in southern Europe show no near-term resolution. At $4,200,000 quantified exposure, the dollar figure is the smallest of the three escalated regions, but the competitive consequence — potential loss of the proprietary maintenance algorithms that differentiate AeroGrid's service contracts in the Mediterranean — is disproportionate to the VaCR figure and persists well beyond the immediate incident.
