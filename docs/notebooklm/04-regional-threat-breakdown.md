# Regional Threat Breakdown — March 2026

## Combined Exposure: $44,700,000 across 3 escalated regions

---

## AME — North America | CRITICAL | $22,000,000

**Threat type:** Ransomware (Financial Impact Rank #1 globally)
**Signal type:** Event — a specific, confirmed incident
**Admiralty rating:** B2 (Reliable source, information probably true)
**Velocity:** Stable

### What happened
A confirmed extortion campaign against a North American wind energy operator. A $22,000,000 ransom demand was issued. A separate but related attack caused 18 days of operational downtime and forced a full network rebuild across three facilities.

### Why AeroGrid is exposed
AeroGrid's live wind farm telemetry and remote maintenance scheduling platforms use the same operational architecture that was successfully attacked. The attack method has already been proven effective against the wind energy sector.

### The regulatory amplifier
A US executive order designating offshore wind as critical national infrastructure means a successful attack triggers mandatory public disclosure obligations on top of the operational damage — raising total consequence beyond the $22M VaCR figure.

### Crown jewel at risk
Live telemetry data from offshore and onshore wind farms + remote maintenance scheduling systems

## APAC — Asia Pacific | HIGH | $18,500,000

**Threat type:** System intrusion (Financial Impact Rank #3 globally)
**Signal type:** Trend — sustained, worsening pattern
**Admiralty rating:** B2 (Reliable source, information probably true)
**Velocity:** Stable

### What's happening
State-directed collection campaigns across Asia-Pacific are specifically targeting wind turbine control systems, predictive maintenance platforms, and blade production infrastructure. The assessed access vector is supply chain compromise through third-party industrial automation software updates.

### Why this is different from AME
This is not financially motivated — it is strategic technology acquisition driven by South China Sea geopolitical competition. The goal is persistent access and IP theft, not operational disruption (yet).

### The escalation risk
If this sustained collection trend transitions from intelligence-gathering into disruptive operations, AeroGrid faces potential interruption to blade manufacturing schedules and permanent erosion of proprietary designs.

### Crown jewel at risk
Proprietary turbine aerodynamic designs + OT/SCADA networks in blade manufacturing plants

## MED — Mediterranean | MEDIUM | $4,200,000

**Threat type:** Insider misuse (Financial Impact Rank #4 globally)
**Signal type:** Mixed — a trend that has materialized into a specific incident
**Admiralty rating:** B2 (Reliable source, information probably true)
**Velocity:** Stable

### The structural driver
Sustained economic austerity across southern Europe is creating financial pressure on field service and maintenance workers — the exact personnel who hold privileged access to predictive maintenance algorithms and offshore servicing contract documentation.

### The confirmed incident
A Mediterranean energy operator experienced a case where a former maintenance contractor retained active credentials for six months post-departure and accessed wind farm telemetry data. This confirms the pathway has already been exploited in the same operating environment as AeroGrid.

### Why the dollar figure understates the risk
$4.2M is the quantified VaCR exposure. But the competitive consequence — potential loss of the proprietary maintenance algorithms that differentiate AeroGrid's service contracts in the Mediterranean market — persists well beyond any single incident and is not fully captured by the dollar figure.

### Crown jewel at risk
Real-time predictive maintenance algorithms + offshore servicing contract documentation
